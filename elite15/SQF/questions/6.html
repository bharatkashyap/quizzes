In 2005, a man called Doug Heckman, while installing utility software for his PC, noticed something in the end user license agreement that 3000 users before him had failed to notice. He called the software company up, enquired about what he had seen, and ended up getting $1,000 richer. What did he find?
To prove that no one really reads EULAs, a utility software maker called 'PC Pitstop' added a 'special consideration' clause to its agreement saying that those who read this section may be 'financially compensated'. It took five months after the release for them to receive their first, and only, call.
