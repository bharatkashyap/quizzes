X is a news summarization app created by teenage English computer programmer Nick D'Aloisio. It used an analytical tool to condense text content into 1000, 500, or 140-character summary text, and aimed to solve perceived problems with the way news articles are presented on smartphones, with the initial version of X being downloaded by over 200,000 users. D'Aloisio received venture capital funding from several international celebrities like Ashton Kutcher, Stephen Fry, Yoko Ono and Lee Ka-Shing. In 2013, X was sold to Yahoo for $30 million.
